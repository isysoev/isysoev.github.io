MAS.S.65

Designing learning technology for children

MIT Media Lab, Spring 2020

This class was organized by my colleague Safinah Ali and I, under the supervision of our mentor Dr. Cynthia Breazeal. We came up with the idea of this class, since during our own journeys of developing technology for children, we realized that it involves a great amount of perspectives from many different fields, and these perspectives are sometimes at odds with each other. Figuring out these contradictions was once very confusing for us, and we wanted to create a class that would spare others the same confusion.

In our class, we combined materials from the fields of learning theory, constructionist learning, design and educational technology practice. You can find our class materials and syllabus here.

Class experience

Our class is partially project-based and is composed of two types of sessions: theoretical ones and design studios. During theoretical sessions, students discuss readings from the relevant fields and build connections to their experience and practice. During design studios, students present updates on the class projects.
