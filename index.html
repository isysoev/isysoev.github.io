Hi, I am Ivan, a postdoctoral associate at the Center for Constructive Communication and the Personal Robots Group at MIT Media Lab. I am focused on designing, developing and studying digital learning technologies - particularly those that promote play and empower the learner. This spring, I'm co-teaching a graduate class Designing learning technologies for children. In addition, I like to draw, paint and write children's stories.

Projects

Here are some representative projects that I worked on - tap on the images to read more!

Child-driven,
machine-guided
MIT Media Lab
Project lead, Concept, Design, Development, Research

Educational approaches such as constructionism rely on child-driven learning activities. But to be effective, child-driven learning requires scaffolding (guidance). And with every learner working on their own project, such guidance can be very labor-intensive. In this project, we attempted to automate it.

Phoneme blocks for building words
MIT Media Lab
Project lead, Concept, Design, Animation, Development, Research

English is notorious for its convoluted relationship between spelling and pronunciation. This complexity makes it difficult to learn certain literacy skills, or spell words sound-by-sound in child-driven learning apps. Here, we attempted to address this issue by using phoneme-based (instead of letter-based) building blocks.

What would you like to spell?
MIT Media Lab
Project lead, Concept, Design, Development, Research

In other projects above, we explored how software can automatically guide children through making words that they would like to spell. But in order to do that, the system first needs to learn what these words are. In this project, we explore various approaches to do that.

Robotic guide for literacy learning
MIT Media Lab
Project lead, Design, Development, Research

Robotic characters allow for more humanlike scaffolding (guidance). In this project, we explore how a robot can help the child to both build words and come up with creative ideas.

Tinkering with words
MIT Media Lab
Project lead, Concept, Design, Development, Research

Children are natural explorers and tinkerers. This explains the popularity of toys like LEGO, or programming tools like Scratch. In this project, we apply a LEGO-like tool to early literacy learning.

Analyzing open-ended learning play
MIT Media Lab
Participated in Design, Development and Research

Analyzing open-ended play with digital learning apps could be difficult precisely because of its richness. In this project, we developed tools that help to discover interesting phenomena in the play data.
